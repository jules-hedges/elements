3.3 Enriched links and colinks

Definition 3.1 Let {\mathcal{C}} and {\mathcal{D}} be categories. A connection {\mathcal{C}} \to {\mathcal{D}} [todo, come up with a better name probably] consists of a monoidal category {\mathcal{M}}, an enrichment of {\mathcal{D}} in {\mathcal{M}}, and a right action of {\mathcal{M}} on {\mathcal{C}}.

Definition 3.2 Let {\mathcal{M}} be a monoidal category. Let {\mathcal{D}} be a category enriched in {\mathcal{M}}, and let {\mathcal{C}} be a category equipped with a right action of {\mathcal{M}}. Let X, Y be objects of {\mathcal{C}} and X', Y' objects of {\mathcal{D}}. An enriched link f : \binom{X'}{X} \to \binom{Y'}{Y} is a morphism f : X \to Y \bullet [X', Y'] in {\mathcal{C}}.

Definition 3.3 Let {\mathcal{M}} be a monoidal category. Let {\mathcal{D}} be a category enriched in {\mathcal{M}}, and let {\mathcal{C}} be a category equipped with a left action of {\mathcal{M}}. Let X, Y be objects of {\mathcal{C}} and X', Y' objects of {\mathcal{D}}. An enriched colink f : \binom{X'}{X} \to \binom{Y'}{Y} is a morphism f : X \to [Y', X'] \bullet Y in {\mathcal{C}}.

Definition 3.4 Let f : \binom{X'}{X} \to \binom{Y'}{Y} and g : \binom{Y'}{Y} \to \binom{Z'}{Z} be enriched links. The composition fg is the enriched link \binom{X'}{X} \to \binom{Z'}{Z} given by X \xrightarrow{f} [X', Y'] \bullet Y \xrightarrow{[X', Y'] \bullet g} [X', Y'] \bullet [Y', X'] \bullet Z \xrightarrow{\cong} [X', Y'] \otimes [Y', Z'] \bullet Z \xrightarrow{\circ \bullet Z} [X', Z'] \bullet Z

Definition 3.5 Let f : \binom{X'}{X} \to \binom{Y'}{Y} and g : \binom{Y'}{Y} \to \binom{Z'}{Z} be enriched colinks. The composition fg is the enriched colink \binom{X'}{X} \to \binom{Z'}{Z} given by X \xrightarrow{f} Y \bullet [Y', X'] \xrightarrow{g \bullet [Y', X']} Z \bullet [Z', Y'] \bullet [Y', X'] \xrightarrow{\cong} Z \bullet [Z', Y'] \otimes [Y', X'] \xrightarrow{Z \bullet \circ} Z \bullet [Z', Y']

3.4 Parametrisation

Definition 3.6 Let {\mathcal{C}} be a category and {\mathcal{M}} a monoidal category with a right action on {\mathcal{C}}. Let X and Y be objects of {\mathcal{C}}. A parametrised morphism f : X \to Y in {\mathcal{C}} consists of an object M of {\mathcal{M}} and a morphism f : X \bullet M \to Y. We call M the parameter object of f.

Definition 3.7 Let {\mathcal{C}} be a category and {\mathcal{M}} a monoidal category with a right action on {\mathcal{C}}.Let f : X \to Y and g : Y \to Z be parametrised morphisms with parameter objects M, N. The composition fg : X \to Z is the parametrised morphism with parameter object M \otimes N given by X \bullet M \otimes N \xrightarrow{\cong} X \bullet M \bullet N \xrightarrow{f \bullet N} Y \bullet N \xrightarrow{g} Z
